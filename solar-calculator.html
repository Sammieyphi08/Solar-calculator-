📘 Formulas & Methodology

☀️ Peak Sun Hours (PSH)

Hours per day of equivalent full-intensity sunlight (1000 W/m²). Nigeria averages 4.5–6.5 hrs. Always use site-specific data where possible (NASA POWER, PVGIS).

🟡 Panel Sizing

Panels = ⌈(Daily Wh ÷ System Eff.) ÷ (Panel W × PSH × Install Factor)⌉

System efficiency accounts for wiring losses, inverter losses, and temperature derating. Typical range: 75–90%.

🔋 Battery Bank Sizing

Batteries = ⌈(Daily Wh × Autonomy Days) ÷ (Bat V × Bat Ah × DoD × Bat Eff.)⌉

Lead-acid DoD: 50% (flooded), 60% (AGM/Gel). Lithium DoD: 80–100%. Always round up to whole batteries.

⚡ Inverter Sizing

Inverter (VA) = Total Connected Load (W) × 1.25 × Power Factor (0.8)

The 1.25× surge factor covers motor startups (fridges, pumps, fans). Always select the next standard size up.

🔌 MPPT Controller

MPPT (A) = (Total Array Watts ÷ System Voltage) × 1.25

The 1.25× factor covers cloud-edge irradiance spikes (up to 1200 W/m²). Check Voc string does not exceed MPPT max input voltage.

🔧 Cable Cross-Section

A (mm²) = (2 × Length × Current) ÷ (σ × Voltage Drop)

σ = conductivity (copper: 56, aluminium: 35). Always select the next standard cable size above the calculated value.

⚠️ Field Best Practices
Add 20–30% panel buffer. Real-world losses (dust, shading, temperature derating, soiling) aren't captured in ideal PSH calculations.
LiFePO4 > Lead-Acid for longevity. LiFePO4 lasts 2,000–6,000 cycles vs 300–600 for flooded lead-acid. Higher upfront cost, lower total cost of ownership.
48V for systems above 2 kW. Lower current = thinner cables, less resistive loss, smaller breakers. Always preferred for commercial installations.
Never mix old and new batteries. Adding new batteries to an existing bank accelerates degradation of both. Replace the full bank at once.
